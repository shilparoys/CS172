Honoring the history-makers

In recognition of Black History Month, USC Athletics presents a series of stories highlighting Trojans who have made history on and off the field.

Immune system can wipe brain of Alzheimer’s indicator

New research from scientists at the Keck School of Medicine of USC shows that the body’s immune system may be able to clear the brain of toxic plaque build-up that is the hallmark of Alzheimer’s disease, reversing memory loss and brain cell damage.

Read the full story at USC News

Signing Day a ringing success for Trojan football squad

When the dust settled Wednesday, Coach Steve Sarkisian and his staff successfully recruited a class that collegiate sports website Rivals.com called the best in the nation. The new Trojans include four five-star recruits, 17 four-star recruits and five three-star recruits.

Read the full story at USC News

To fight HIV among homeless, researchers turn to math

USC researchers have teamed up with Los Angeles social workers to address the public-health problem using an unlikely tool: mathematics. Scientists created an algorithm that determines the best person to spread the message, resulting in about 60 percent more information being distributed.
